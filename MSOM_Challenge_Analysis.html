  • Work Cell GEA CDC-50 RIG (Work Cell?)

Raw Materials:
- Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API),
- Diluents: Microcrystalline Ceullulose & Anhydrous Dicalcium Phosphate
- Disintegrants: Crosarmellose Sodium
- Lubricants: Magnesium Stearate & Sodium Stearyl Fumarate

Process:
- API, Diluents & Disintegrants are fed into a continuous linear blender, Blender 1, which comproises 96% of the tablet formulation.
-The outlet of Blender 1 feed directly into the second continuous linear blender, Blender 2, and here the Lubricants are also fed into the blender completing the remaining 4% of formulation.
- The outlet of Blender 2 is fed past a Blend NIR Unit and into a rotary Tablet Press
- Measurements collected at: NIR UNIT OUTSIDE of BLENDER 2 & TABLET HPLC

#### Residence Time Distribution t: Time C(t) Concentration of API measured as a function of Time for all the experiments:
E(t) Probability Density Function for all Units: \(E(t) = \frac{C(t)}{\int^{\infty}_0 C(t)dt}\)
Mean Residence Time (MRT): \(\int^{\infty}_0 tE(t)dt\)
PFR RTD: \(\E_{plug}(t) = \delta(t-\theta)\)
Dirac Delta Function: \(\delta\) Mean Residence Time of PFR (Delay Time): \(\theta\)

LiW Feeders

  • 5 Total Loss in Weight Feeders (PD1, PD2, PD3, PD4, PD5, PD7).

  • Loss in Weight Feeders have been designed to feed the blenders with a predetermined amount of raw materials at the correct ratio and the right pace.

  • It is a gravimetric feeder that directly measures the raw materials weight to maintain their flow at a predetermined rate.

  • Product is dispensed at a constant loss of weight per time unit, such that the material contained in the hopper is delivered by the feeder at a constant rate. The feed rate is obtained by measuring the reduction of the systems weight over time.

  • The control of the feed rate is accomplished by comparing the rate of the actual weight loss of the measured system to the set point and hence adjusting the motor speed accordingly.

  • The proportion of the individual feed rates determines the concentration of the different materials in the final product recipe

  • Residence Time Distribution (RTD): Probability Distribution Function describing the amount of time a single particle spends in the system.

  • RTD establishes an understanding of the relationship between operating conditions and the mean residence time of the system. In order to optimize a RTD model, there must be the inclusion of axial dispersion or tanks-in-series and may need to leverage various convolution techniques.

  • Residence Time Distribution has been utilized in powder processing, circulating fluidized beds, and continuous horizontal blenders.

  • Continuous Manufacturing relies heavily upon the use of Near-Infrared Spectroscopy for blend composition monitoring. NIR Spectroscopy is related and relies on near-infrared light being used to characterize materials. NIR Spectroscopy is the analytical technique of using near-infrared radiation to analyze samples for compositional or characteristic traits.

  • PLACES WHERE MEASUREMENTS WERE TAKEN IN 1. The blend, via the blend NIR unit at the outlet of Blender 2 and (2). The Tablets, via tablet HPLC
